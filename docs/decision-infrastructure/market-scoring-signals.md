# Market Scoring Signals

Market Scoring Signals are the objective inputs used to evaluate opportunity quality and risk.

## Timing Signals (Entry/Exit Windows)

### Goal

Identify when market conditions favor entry, hold, or exit decisions.

### Signal examples

- Momentum and trend inflections
- Inventory compression/expansion
- Pricing acceleration vs. fundamentals

## Stress Resilience (Grade A-F)

### Goal

Assess how well a market segment withstands adverse conditions.

### Signal examples

- Drawdown behavior under stress periods
- Liquidity and absorption stability
- Volatility and recovery speed

## Verified Gross Yield

### Goal

Provide auditable, apples-to-apples return estimates anchored in verified inputs.

### Signal examples

- Contracted rent coverage
- Occupancy-normalized gross yield
- Yield variance across comparable cohorts

## Data Confidence Level

### Goal

Expose trust and completeness to qualify recommendation strength.

### Signal examples

- Source agreement and reconciliation score
- Field-level completeness score
- Freshness and latency profile

## Scoring governance

- Publish clear weighting rules for each signal.
- Version score formulas to preserve historical explainability.
- Store per-score evidence references for auditability.
- Separate objective market score from profile-fit score.
